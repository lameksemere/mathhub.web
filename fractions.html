Understanding Fractions

Ages 6–10 · 3 Levels

A fraction is a part of a whole. When you cut a pizza into 4 equal slices and eat 1 slice, you've eaten ¼ of the pizza. The number on top is the numerator (your part) and the number on the bottom is the denominator (total parts).

1
4

1 out of 4 equal parts

Numerator

The top number — how many parts you have

Denominator

The bottom number — total equal parts

Easy Level — For Ages 6–7

Identify simple fractions and find parts of shapes.

A pizza is cut into 4 equal slices. You eat 1 slice. What fraction did you eat?

A chocolate bar has 8 pieces. You eat 4. What fraction did you eat?

Colour 2 out of 6 boxes. What fraction is coloured?

A ribbon is cut into 2 equal pieces. What fraction is one piece?

There are 3 apples in a basket of 9 fruits. What fraction are apples?

5 out of 10 children have hats. What fraction have hats?

lightbulb Tips & Key Rules

🍕

Think of Pizza!

Fractions are just equal slices of something. 1/4 = one slice when the pizza has 4 equal slices.

compress

Simplify!

2/4 = 1/2 · 3/9 = 1/3 · Divide both numbers by the same number to simplify.

add

Same Bottom = Easy Add

1/5 + 2/5 = 3/5. When denominators are the same, just add the tops!

cake

Whole Number Rule

When the top equals the bottom (4/4, 6/6), it equals 1 — the whole thing!
